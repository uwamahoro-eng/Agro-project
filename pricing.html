Do You know?

Tractors are essential machines in agriculture, designed to perform a variety of tasks that enhance farming efficiency.

Here are some key roles they play: Tilling: They are used with tillers to break up soil and mix in organic material, improving soil structure. Planting: Specialized attachments allow tractors to plant seeds in rows at the appropriate depth and spacing. Harvesting: Some tractors can be equipped with harvesters or trailers to collect crops efficiently. Transporting: Tractors are often used to haul loads of crops, equipment, or supplies across the farm. Mowing and Haymaking: Tractors can pull mowers to cut grass or hay, which is essential for livestock feed. Fertilizing and Spraying: Tractors can carry tanks for applying fertilizers or pesticides, ensuring even distribution over large areas. Cultivating: They are used with cultivators to control weeds and improve soil conditions between rows of crops.
